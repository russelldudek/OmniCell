Architecture-to-Field Tolerance Stack

The system model: six decisions that accumulate into product scalability.

LayerTechnical questionMinimum evidenceDownstream consequence
1. Clinical intentWhat patient, pharmacy, nursing, or technician outcome must improve?Workflow context, hazard/need, user burden, acceptance logicPrevents feature novelty from substituting for value
2. Platform architectureWhat becomes common, configurable, isolated, or product-specific?Interfaces, decision rights, compatibility, architecture principlesControls reuse, upgrade, and configuration complexity
3. Mechanism + softwareHow do mechanics, sensing, control, software, and cloud behavior interact?Failure modes, tolerance sensitivity, observability, recoveryDetermines performance and diagnosability
4. Verification evidenceWhat evidence is enough for the intended release scope?Representative conditions, regression, traceability, residual riskSeparates confidence from optimism
5. IndustrializationCan the system be built, sourced, installed, trained, and serviced repeatedly?Build readiness, supplier controls, install/service playbooks, economicsTurns a design into a scalable product
6. Service learningHow does field signal become product and architecture learning?Normalized data, reproducibility, containment, root cause, recurrence testPrevents recurring fixes from becoming permanent service load
Technical authority operating cadence
Before decision
Expose the tradeoff, decision owner, evidence standard, downstream consumers, and reversibility.
At decision
Resolve, hold, bound, or escalate with explicit rationale and release scope.
After decision
Observe field/build evidence, close recurrence, and convert learning into architecture rules.
Cost of inaction

When the tolerance stack is implicit, programs can optimize locally while exporting late rework, mixed configurations, installation burden, field recurrence, service capacity consumption, and unclear accountability. This is a role hypothesis grounded in the posting’s emphasis on scale, reliability, quality, and cross-functional execution—not a claim about Omnicell’s current state.
