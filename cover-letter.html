The opportunity in this role is to make those decisions visible across a highly matrixed organization so technical authority improves execution rather than becoming another layer of review.

My record is grounded in adjacent, consequential systems. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, root cause, and process controls in advanced electronics and high-reliability technical programs. Amazon added 24/7 launch discipline and customer-backward operating mechanisms; ZeusVu added regulated autonomous-systems judgment and a 100% safety record with no FAA incidents.

I would bring that evidence to Omnicell with the discipline to learn pharmacy context from pharmacists, nurses, technicians, service teams, quality leaders, and customers. The goal would not be to import an external playbook. It would be to help establish the technical contract the portfolio needs: clear architecture boundaries, visible tradeoffs, sufficient evidence, manufacturing and service readiness, and a closed path from field signal back to product decision.

My independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/OmniCell/ makes that reasoning inspectable through an interactive Architecture-to-Field Tolerance Stack, a first-120-day plan, and an Integrity Review.
